# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Target User
The exact target user is a **VP of Engineering or fractional CTO at a Series A/B startup** (20–100 employees). They are currently experiencing pressure from the CFO to reduce SaaS bloat as burn rate scrutiny increases, but they don't want to take tools away from their developers. 

## The "Right Before" Moment
Right before they want this tool, they are looking at a Rippling or Ramp expense report exported to a CSV, filtering by "Software", and realizing they have 45 seats of GitHub Copilot and 20 individual Cursor Pro subscriptions being expensed simultaneously. They search: *"how to track developer AI spend"* or *"cursor vs copilot enterprise ROI"*.

## Where They Hang Out
- **Slack Communities:** `CTO Craft`, `Rands Leadership Slack`, `Techstars Alumni Network`.
- **X (Twitter):** Following accounts like Gergely Orosz (Pragmatic Engineer), Addy Osmani, and VCs talking about AI ROI.
- **Subreddits:** `r/ExperiencedDevs` and `r/SaaS`.

## 30-Day Zero-Budget Acquisition Plan
To get the first 100 users, I will execute a "Reverse Audit" outreach strategy:
1. **Find the targets:** Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator (free trial) to find "Fractional CTO" or "VP Engineering" at startups that recently raised a Series A (using Crunchbase free data).
2. **The Hook:** Direct message them on LinkedIn: *"Hey [Name], I noticed [Company] is scaling the engineering team. I built a free calculator that checks if you're accidentally double-paying for developer AI tools (Cursor + Copilot overlap is costing the average Series A about $8k/yr). Takes 60 seconds, no login required. Worth a look before your next finance sync?"*
3. **The Micro-Community Drop:** Instead of a generic "Launch", post a specific teardown in `r/ExperiencedDevs`: *"I audited $50k of dev AI spend. Here's why 40% of it was completely wasted."* The post breaks down the redundancy math (Rule 2 of the audit engine) and links the tool at the end.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel
**Credex's existing rejected leads.** Credex likely has a pipeline of companies that said "we're not big enough for an enterprise contract yet" or "we don't spend enough on AI to warrant optimization." Credex can blast this free tool to that dead pipeline. It reactivates the lead by providing immediate value, and automatically surfaces the ones whose spend *has* actually grown large enough to qualify for Credex.

## Week-1 Traction
If successful, Week 1 looks like:
- 400 unique visitors.
- 15% completion rate of the form (60 audits generated).
- 20 emails captured.
- 3 high-savings audits ($500+/mo) that click the "Optimize with Credex" CTA.
